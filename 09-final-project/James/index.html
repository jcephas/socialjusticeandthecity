During the spring season of 2021, the majority of the population stayed and lived most of their lives at their homes, regardless of the differences in the types of housing and lifestyles. People who are able to keep working at homes have the alternatives to work and to live without risking their presence out there with the ongoing pandemic which had caused an increased unemployment rate along with increased real estate value. The situation is complicated – with the increasing number of cases of the Covid-19, the value of houses with lands increased while the rent for apartments went down, but considering the unemployment rate, the market was disordered by the occurring pandemic.

The occurrence of the pandemic is unfortunate for all people, but one could see how people can be affected on many different levels based on how and where they live. Objectively, people tend not to live in the apartments due to the shared spaces and utilities such as gym or elevator, so the demand for single-family housing went up. But single-family housing can be expensive, especially within dense cities such as the Boston area. In 2021, the current median price is $557,000 which is $227,000 more than the national median price. The year-to-year increased more than 10% as high demand versus low inventory leads to a bidding war which is incredible to think of during a global pandemic.
