Lived Borders: Tangibility in Intangibility

Consequential geographies are produced from the force of policy and power. As a result, the effects on health, safety, and security deteriorate the quality of life within the landscape. Movement of people and resources through these landscapes are defined by the underlying power systems that create them. The overarching power imbalance also pits those within the marginal borders against one another, defining areas of social vulnerability. Our landscapes are dissected and degraded by intangible borders.
