In The Pruit Igoe Myth, former residents of the complex reflected on their adjustments to living there.

One resident shared that, with few resources to get school supplies for her children, she adapted by turning one wall into a chalkboard. Another resident recalled how playing music from her record player became a community focal point. These are ways that residents took a space and within it, created a sense of place, home, and community.
