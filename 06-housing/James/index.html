Interactive Analysis 06 - Housing

ARCH 6340 - Instructor Prof. Jay Cephas

The Pruitt-Igoe project is always known as an example of inadequate execution in the urban planning aspect. The over-designed structures coming along with supplemented utilities requires consistent maintenances which demands fiscal costs throughout times. However, it was meant to be a public housing accommodating low-income families with enhanced living environments, and ultimately everything fell down quickly.

The project was meant to be a new community to have low-income families enjoy a rather modern and welcoming environment regardless of any group identities such as races or labels. However, with the rapid dissolvent of utilities and supplements of security and hygiene, the residencies were starting to move out. The people who could leave, left while everything was still working rather intact, but the remainder of the residency was not able to leave that easily. The reality of the result was thought-provoking that most of the remaining residency were black residents, and they stayed not because they wanted to but there were no alternatives for them to choose. Although with all the negative opinions on Pruitt-Igoe from outsiders, some of the former residents still viewed their living times within the buildings as one of the best memories of their lives.

The failure of Pruitt-Igoe was merely a partial reflection on the housing segregations that happened in America back in its times. Learning from some previous lectures and readings on the subject, one could realize the colored groups were often being treated differently than the whites on purchasing and making homes in a well-developed neighborhood, and the intentions were coming from both objective policies and subjective bias from white communities. The fact that after black families put down all their efforts and savings on purchasing an estate in a fine community which was mainly composed of white residents, and the white families would intentionally move out of that community thus causing a decrease in quality and value of the neighborhood was truly the most unspoken tragical scenario which no one could ever understand apart from themselves.
