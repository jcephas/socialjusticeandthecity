ARCH 6340 - Instructor Prof. Jay Cephas

This week’s materials are very intriguing onto what architecture is to every individual in this industry or profession. The readings discussed a lot about Architecture as a subject regarding to its past, presents, and future in terms of the relationship between the architects and their projects which can be thoroughly different. The video Citizen Architect, however, presents an example of Samuel “Sambo” Mockbee’s dedication on his understandings of architecture, which resulted in the existence of the Rural Studio in Auburn University.

There was no recondite puzzle or complicated reasoning behind Sambo’s motivation to establish the Studio in a way that puts out students and faculties into the actual communities and have them to communicate and to interact with the local residents, but his position was clear – to build things to the local communities that no commercially-driven architectural firms would. Extracting from the video and the interview on Sambo, the essential idea on architecture is dramatically different that there is no definitive right or wrong, which sometimes causes confusions for people to understand the true purpose of the subject.

“…it changed our whole scope of thinking. It has a humanitarian side to it…” said by one of the interviewees Peanut Robinson, who explained the shift in his attitude and perspective on architecture and architects, because he witnessed the changes and efforts the Studio dedicated in his community. Compared with the video, the second article by Emery Roth discusses the evolving identities for architects that some of them were becoming the “trendsetters” and “media stars”, which is something different from what Samuel was doing with the Rural Studio. From an neutral point of view, the subject of architecture can be delivered with various forms and goals, and the beneficiaries will also be correspondingly different, but the essential value is to fulfill the needs from either the wealthy individual or the rundown communities.
