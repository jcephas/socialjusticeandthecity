Synopsis

Writings on Cities, by Henri Lefebvre, helps readers to understand contemporary life through philosophical and political understanding. The City Repair Project, by Stuart Cowan, Mark Lakeman, Jenny Leis, Daniel Lerch and Jan Semenza, is a specific example of a successful implementation of community involvement within an urban context. Both readings, however, deal with the common element of how best to understand a city, and the importance of knowledge. To seek and bring social justice to fruition, is to seek understandment through constant refinement and the garnering of knowledge; evolution.
