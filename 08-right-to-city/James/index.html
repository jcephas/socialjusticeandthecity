People and their Cities

From previous readings, we learned much about the relation between people and their belonging landscape, and how the proposition of landscape had changed overtimes. For this module, with Lefebvre's Right to the city and Cowan's City repair project, these readings discussed further conceptions and samples regarding the correlation between people and cities.

In Lefebvre's article, he criticized and discussed the obstructions throughout the development and redevelopment phases of urban environments. He classified some of the obstacles as

“mummified and embalmed corpse”

which weighs heavily among the road for the ongoing urbanization process. He pointed out many cities, in this case, were historically constructed yet no longer being alive for nowadays' practicality, which now was mostly consumed by cultural activities for tourists. Moreover, he considered the political and social influences on urban development that the working class is the vital component when taken into considerations in urban contexts.

Speaking of urban contexts, the City repair project written by Cowan focused more on the actual practices been achieved among the city of Portland in Oregon. The City repair project is a non-profit organization with initiatives to transform street intersections back to public spaces for the best of the neighborhood. Similar to the previous case from Can Masdeu, these movements were trying to establish more connections and responsibilities between the urban neighborhoods with their correlated residents and communities. By coordinating these activities, the organization hopes it would revisit the relation between people and built urban contexts among modern cities.
